Cramp Relief
heat, movement & medicine tips
›
Heat therapy: A heating pad on your lower belly for 15–20 min relaxes uterine muscles — as effective as ibuprofen for many people.

Child's pose: Kneel, stretch arms forward, breathe deeply, hold 30 seconds. Releases lower back tension.

Cat-cow: On all fours, alternate arching and rounding your spine — 10 slow reps.

Ibuprofen tip: Works best if taken at the very first sign of cramps — before the pain peaks.

Warm drinks: Ginger tea, chamomile, or warm lemon water reduce inflammation and bloating.
🍫
Food & Cravings
what your body actually needs
›
Dark chocolate: Magnesium in 70%+ cacao genuinely reduces cramps and lifts mood. Enjoy it guilt-free.

Iron-rich foods: Spinach, lentils, pumpkin seeds, red meat — replenish what you lose during your period.

Avoid: Salty snacks (worsen bloating), caffeine (can amplify cramps), alcohol.

Eat: Bananas (potassium), salmon (omega-3 is anti-inflammatory), oats (stabilize blood sugar).

Craving carbs? Serotonin is dipping — complex carbs like oats or sweet potato are great choices.
🧠
Mood & Mental Health
PMS, emotions & coping
›
It's hormonal, not you: Progesterone and estrogen both drop sharply before your period. That causes real anxiety, tearfulness, and irritability. You're not broken.

5-4-3-2-1 grounding: Name 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste. Brings you back to the present.

Rest is allowed: Canceling plans during your period is self-care, not failure.

Crying is good: Emotional release has a measurable physiological calming effect.
💤
Sleep During Your Period
rest better, feel better
›
Best sleep position: Fetal (curled on your side) reduces cramping pressure on your uterus.

Magnesium before bed: A supplement or Epsom salt bath can ease nighttime cramps significantly.

Cool your room: 65–68°F / 18–20°C improves sleep quality — especially when you tend to run warm.

Lavender: Aromatherapy (pillow spray or diffuser) is clinically shown to reduce period-related anxiety.

Screens: Blue light worsens hormonal disruption. Try to put your phone down 45–60 min before sleep.
🧘
Gentle Movement
when exercise actually helps
›
Day 1–2: Rest or very light yoga only. Listen to your body — don't push through heavy pain.

Day 3–4: Light walking or stretching. Movement releases endorphins that naturally reduce pain.

Avoid: Intense HIIT or heavy lifting on high-flow days — it can worsen cramping.

Swimming: Water pressure can temporarily ease flow and cramps. Even a short swim helps.

Yin yoga: Poses held 3–5 min (like supported bridge or legs up the wall) are deeply restorative.
🩸
Period Product Guide
pads, tampons, cups & more
›
Pads: Best for overnight, beginners, or postpartum. Change every 4–6 hours.

Tampons: Great for active days. Change every 4–8 hours max — TSS risk increases beyond that.

Menstrual cups: Eco-friendly, holds 3× more than a tampon, lasts up to 12 hours. Small learning curve but life-changing.

Menstrual discs: Can be worn during sex. Very flat, comfortable for all-day wear.

Period underwear: Sustainable, washable backup or perfect for light flow days.
☎️
When to See a Doctor
symptoms that deserve attention
›
See a healthcare provider if you experience:

• Cramps so severe they stop you from daily activities
• Bleeding so heavy you soak a pad/tampon every hour for 2+ hours
• Periods lasting longer than 7 days
• Irregular or missed periods (when not due to pregnancy)
• Severe PMS or PMDD that significantly impacts your mental health
• Pain during sex or between periods

These can be signs of conditions like endometriosis, PCOS, or fibroids — all very treatable when caught early. You deserve care. 💜
